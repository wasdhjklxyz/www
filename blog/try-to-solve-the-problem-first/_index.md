# Try to Solve the Problem First!
<time datetime="2026-06-14">Jun 14, 2026</time>

Try the problem first! Some of my tech literate friends try to push vibe coding
and AI onto me everytime I talk to them about software and it drives me insane!
I feel like many of these people have lost the true craftsmanship spirit of what
made software development/engineering great in the first place.

They'll tell me something like "dude but with these new models now its so much
better and good" or "manually writing software is a waste of time." While I have
vibe coded before (not proud of it) I still read and understand what the AI is
telling me to do.

I'd much rather spend my time with the AI learning about new ways to implement
things or having it point out mistakes of mine when I ask it for a review. Its
not just a debugger powered by an LLM, its an advanced search engine.

But this brings me to the point of this text, I don't think AI should be the
first thing you go to when solving a problem. For example, I'm working on a
[job posting funnel](https://github.com/wasdhjklxyz/job-finder) and have a list
of TODOs.

I almost caught myself just going to the AI to be like "how do I parse the JSON
output and remove promoted jobs." I'm sure it would provide a result that works
but my point is this makes me feel like illiterate or told what to do and I
really don't like that way of thinking.

I think the craftsmanship in writing and understanding software sort of makes it
ritualistic and more enjoyable in the long term reflection on projects. I'd much
rather look back on this project and have that memory of reading documentation
and being like "oH!" figuring out the workings of the library or language
(learning Rust) that I'm working with than having someone hand me the answer
everytime I'm faced with a challenge.

I'm no psychology expert but this definetly does better things to my brain for
learning and self achievement as well. So if you're reading this and vibe code
or use AI assistence during programming, before you tackle a problem, think to
yourself "can I figure this out on my own what would I do first." You'll know
exactly what I'm asking of you when you ask yourself this question. Try it!
