Against the Second Monitor

Jul, 9 2026

Today I decided to turn off my second monitor and it was great for focus. Normally, I’ll have my browser and terminal (emulator) open with each taking up an entire display (sometimes splitting my second display vertically for music).

By only using one monitor, my eyes never drifted over if I got bored or to some sort of stutter/stop in my work. This prevents the “getting distracted” problem of having two monitors that you might’ve heard before.

However, my favorite advantage* of one monitor is the increased effort needed to view another window. Having whatever I am working on fullscreen means I’d have to alt+tab to view another window. The greater physical effort locks my brain in sort of “purist mode” where I tend to seek information in ways that are already at my fingertips with my Neovim1 workflow.

For example, today I spent my time learning how nixd2 works by tracing the source code and callstack(s) of what I was looking at. This allowed me to efficiently read documentation, jump around the project, and edit files all without waiting for web pages to load.

This can be argued as “yeah that’s why nvim is goated bro” regardless of a second monitor’s state. While I do agree with this, I want to emphasize that there’s a certain beauty of doing everything within the terminal (extra points if only one tmux3 pane) that goes away if you have a second window visible.

This workflow inherently reduces the “drug” that is AI being able to quickly present whatever information you’re seeking in whatever format you want. We all know the effects on our learning process that AI has no reason to go deeper than that…

Throughout most of my time at school, this was how I worked every day. In fact, I was almost never in my apartment/dorm while working/programming and spent most of my time in a lab, study room, or at a friend’s place.

This forced me to use my laptop, which has a 14" 1600x900 230 Nit LED backlight display4. Even if I had the luxury of another monitor to connect to, I would never use my laptop as a second display.

The external display I did connect to was 16:10 and was PERFECT for splitting two 80 char width windows with line numbers and sign-columns5 at a comfortable font size (I still want one to this day).

Once I graduated, I returned home to a proper setup and used both my monitors since I forgot how useful it could be. I won’t go into the benefits there, that’s not what this blog is about but I find myself drifting back and forth between one monitor and two monitor usage. I rediscovered why I love using only one monitor and that led me to write this blog post.
